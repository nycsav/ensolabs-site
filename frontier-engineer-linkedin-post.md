I'm in New York this week for Tech Week, and every conversation keeps landing on the same question: who is going to actually deploy all of this AI into production?

In May alone:

Anthropic launched a $1.5B joint venture with Blackstone, Goldman Sachs, and Hellman & Friedman to embed engineers inside companies deploying Claude.

OpenAI launched a $4B+ Deployment Company backed by 19 investors and acquired Tomoro — bringing 150 forward-deployed engineers on board from day one.

Accenture, ServiceNow, Microsoft, and EY all launched dedicated forward-deployed engineering practices.

FDE job postings are up 729% year-over-year. Total comp at frontier labs starts at $385K mid-level and clears $1M at principal.

The message from the entire industry: the bottleneck in enterprise AI is not the model. It's the deployment. And the person who closes that gap is a forward-deployed engineer — someone who embeds inside your operations, understands your workflows, and ships the system. Not a slide. The system.

This is what we've been doing at Enso Labs since 2025. Embedded with a Fortune 500 manufacturer to ship a 731-document market intelligence pipeline. Built a pharma AI Center of Excellence that compressed campaign launches from 3 months to 2 weeks under full MLR compliance. Ran an autonomous trading terminal in production for over a year on Claude + MCP.

The forward-deployed engineer is not a new role. Palantir proved the model a decade ago. What's new is that every major AI company and every Big 4 firm just bet billions that it's the future of enterprise deployment.

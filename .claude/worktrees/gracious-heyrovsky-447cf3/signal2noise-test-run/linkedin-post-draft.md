## LinkedIn Post (Sonnet 4.6 editorial voice rewrite)

75% of paying Perplexity users now use Finance. Anthropic just shipped 10 dedicated finance agents. The $17.9B generative AI in financial services market is splitting in real time — and the two companies are making opposite bets on how to win it.

Perplexity is building a bring-your-own-data platform: 40+ live finance tools, 35 prebuilt analyst workflows, and integration with Morningstar, PitchBook, Daloopa, and Carbon Arc. The model: connect to whatever data licenses your firm already pays for and automate the analyst's daily tasks around them.

Anthropic is going deeper and narrower: 10 agent templates for pitchbook generation, credit memos, KYC, month-end close, and insurance claims — each packaged as skills + connectors + subagents. The model: autonomous agents that run complex, multi-hour workflows end-to-end, backed by a $1.5B PE joint venture with Blackstone and Goldman Sachs.

What this means for enterprise finance leaders: you're about to be sold two fundamentally different visions. One says "we'll make your existing stack smarter." The other says "we'll replace the workflow entirely."

The question isn't which platform is better. It's which one matches how your organization actually makes decisions — augmented analysts or autonomous agents.

Your next move: map your top 5 most labor-intensive finance workflows. For each one, ask: does this need a faster analyst, or does it need to run without one? That answer determines which architecture you should be evaluating.

Anthropic and Perplexity both launched finance-specific AI platforms last week. Wall Street noticed — FactSet dropped 8%, Morningstar fell 3%.

But buried in the coverage is a more important signal.

The signal: CIO.com reports that Gartner now predicts 70% of enterprises will abandon agentic AI solutions from forward-deployed engineer engagements by 2028 — because they can't evolve them independently. Anthropic's own finance agent rollout requires embedding a dozen FDEs per client. Analyst Nik Kale put it bluntly: "CIOs thought they were buying software. They're actually buying a professional services engagement."

Meanwhile, 44% of finance teams are already using agentic AI (up 600% from 2025), enterprise token costs dropped 67% year-over-year, and only 23% report meaningful ROI from AI agents. The models got cheaper. The implementation didn't.

The Enso take: We built a financial AI trading terminal on Claude before Anthropic shipped finance agents. The hardest part was never the model — it was designing systems that the team could operate, inspect, and evolve without us in the room. That's the difference between a demo and a deployment. The enterprises pulling ahead aren't the ones with the best models. They're the ones who built for independence from day one.

Your Monday move: Audit your current AI agent deployments. For each one, ask: "If the vendor's engineers disappeared tomorrow, could my team maintain this?" If the answer is no, you don't have a product — you have a dependency.

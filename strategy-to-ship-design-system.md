# Strategy to Ship — Brand & Design System (v1)

### A media publishing brand from Enso Labs
*Design brief and principles — June 2026. Supersedes the `signal2noise` brand. Companion to `design-philosophy.md` (Enso Labs "Systematic Resonance").*

---

## 0. What this is

**Strategy to Ship** is the name and identity of Enso Labs' media publishing engine — the product previously developed as `signal2noise` / `PlannerAPI`. It is the system that turns raw inputs (frontier news + uploaded docs + financial data) into ranked signals, an insight, and platform-native stories, with a human at the approval gate.

This document defines a **separate-but-related brand identity** for that product: its own name, logotype, type, color, imagery, voice, and format templates — endorsed by, but visually distinct from, Enso Labs. Enso Labs is the **studio** (cool, structural, mission-control). Strategy to Ship is the **publication** (warm, editorial, human). They are family; they are not the same brand.

> **Naming:** the studio's tagline is already *"Strategy to Ship."* Adopting it as the publishing brand turns a tagline into a product — strong continuity, zero new vocabulary to teach the market.

---

## 1. Positioning

**Strategy to Ship is where strategy becomes production — the field notes of a studio that ships.**

- **Category:** a practitioner media brand at the intersection of **media × advertising × AI**.
- **Promise:** we don't comment on the frontier from the outside; we build with it and report from the room.
- **Mix:** *media* (editorial rigor, sourced, formats people actually read) × *advertising* (art direction, bold headlines, campaign-ready) × *AI* (explainable, multi-channel, produced by a governed engine with a human gate).
- **Feel:** **modern but human.** Modern = the machine signal (mono metadata, crisp accent, clean grid, the engine behind it). Human = warm paper, an editorial serif, real photography of real rooms and people, a named human-in-the-loop.
- **Endorsement line:** *"Strategy to Ship — a publishing engine from Enso Labs."* The footer credit **"Powered by Enso Labs"** is retained.

---

## 2. The design philosophy: "Warm Signal"

Enso Labs is *Systematic Resonance* — mission control at midnight. Strategy to Ship is its complement: **Warm Signal** — the morning the work ships.

1. **The page is human; the engine is modern.** Every surface reads first as something a person made and stands behind — warm paper, an editorial serif, a byline, a photograph of an actual room. The AI shows up as *signal*, not spectacle: a mono version tag, a confidence note, a sourced link, the quiet evidence of a machine that did the legwork. Never a glowing brain, never a robot.

2. **Advertising craft, editorial substance.** A headline earns the stop (advertising); the body earns the trust (media). Big type, decisive crops, one idea per frame — then sourced, practitioner substance underneath. The art direction is bold; the claims are footnoted.

3. **The arrow is the brand.** `→` is *strategy **to** ship* — the vector from idea to production. It is the logotype's hinge, the recurring device between states (strategy → ship, draft → live, signal → story), and the spiritual sibling of Enso's forward-leaning chevron. Where Enso descends (cascading chevrons, foundation), Strategy to Ship advances (a single arrow, momentum).

4. **Built for the format, not the canvas.** This brand lives as a LinkedIn carousel, an X card, a Substack header, a newsletter banner, an OG image, an `/insights` article — not as one hero composition. The system is a **kit of format templates** that all carry the same DNA at every aspect ratio and every scale, from a 1080 square in a feed to a full-bleed article hero.

## 3. Logotype

- **Primary wordmark:** `Strategy → Ship` set in **Lora** (medium/semibold), with the **→ in Ship Coral**. The arrow is the only colored element — it is the signal.
- **Lowercase handle:** `strategy → ship` — for social handles, URLs, casual placements (mirrors the old `signal2noise` lowercase ease).
- **Monogram:** **`S→S`** — a compact mark for avatars, favicons, stamps. The arrow stays coral.
- **Endorsement lockup:** wordmark + a hairline + mono `FROM ENSO LABS`.
- **Clear space & scale:** minimum height 18px for the wordmark; the monogram must survive 32px. The arrow's coral must never drop out — it is the recognition cue.
- **Don't:** don't set the wordmark in a sans, don't recolor the arrow anything but coral, don't add a glow, don't pair it with Enso's chevron in the same lockup (sibling brands, separate signatures).

## 4. Color

A warm, paper-and-ink editorial palette with one decisive accent. Distinct from Enso's electric navy/teal; teal is retained only as the parent-brand link.

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| **Paper** | `#F7F1E6` | Primary reading ground (warm newsprint) |
| **Paper 2** | `#EEE5D3` | Cards, insets, table fills |
| **Ink** | `#1E1813` | Body text; the dark ground for cards/OG |
| **Ink Deep** | `#16110B` | Deepest ground for social/OG cards |
| **Ship Coral** | `#F0512E` | The signature signal — arrow, stamps, pull-quotes, CTAs, "today" |
| **Ledger Amber** | `#E0A23C` | Secondary warm accent (heritage nod to signal2noise) |
| **Slate** | `#79705F` | Muted metadata, secondary text |
| **Line** | `#DDD2BC` | Hairlines, dividers, table borders |
| **Enso Teal** | `#5CE0D2` | Parent-brand link only — *"from Enso Labs"*, used sparingly |

**Rules:** Coral is a signal, not a fill — used at ~5–10% of any composition (the arrow, a stamp, one highlighted word). Never tint large fields coral. On dark grounds (Ink Deep), text is Paper and the accent stays Coral. Teal appears only in the Enso endorsement, never as a headline accent.

## 5. Typography

A three-voice system: a human serif, a modern sans, a machine mono.

- **Display / headlines — Lora.** A warm, contemporary serif with real character. This is the "human + media" voice. Use for article titles, OG headlines, pull-quotes. Weights 500–700, tight leading (1.05–1.15), generous size (the headline is the art).
- **Body / UI — Inter Tight.** Shared with Enso (family DNA), modern and clean. Article body, captions, buttons, navigation. 400–500.
- **Metadata / signal — JetBrains Mono.** The machine voice. Kickers, datelines, version tags, source labels, the engine's confidence notes. 11–14px, uppercase, letter-spaced. This is where the "AI" reads.

**Hierarchy:** mono kicker (small, spaced) → Lora headline (large) → Inter Tight body. The jump from tiny mono to large serif is the signature contrast — machine annotation locating a human story.

---

## 6. Imagery & graphic language

- **Photography (human):** warm-graded documentary photography — real people in real rooms (the OpenAI Builder Lounge shots are the template). Decisive crops, type allowed to sit over the image. Treatment: slight warm grade, deep contrast, paper-toned. **Never** stock "AI" imagery, glowing brains, robots, or neural-net clipart.
- **Graphic system (modern/AI):** the **strategy → ship lane** (a dotted route from a "strategy" node to a "ship" node), **version stamps** (`v5`, `SHIPPED`, `LIVE` as inked rubber-stamp marks in coral), and a **release ledger** (a dated, mono list — like the OG sidebar). These are the "engine made visible."
- **The stamp motif:** a coral, slightly-rotated rubber-stamp (`SHIPPED`, `IN PRODUCTION`, `v5`) is the brand's most ownable advertising device — human craft (a physical stamp) meeting machine output.

---

## 7. Voice

Practitioner editorial, advertising-sharp, AI-honest. (Carries over the article voice already in production.)

- **Headlines:** plain-English, specific, no jargon ("frontier model" → "AI model" for a general feed), no hype words (revolutionary, game-changing, transformative).
- **Body:** first-person studio "we"; sourced claims with inline links; buyer-translation ("what this means for a CTO").
- **Tone:** confident, warm, plainspoken. Modern but human means: a smart colleague who ships, not a vendor who pitches.
- **Always:** name the human-in-the-loop. End with **Powered by Enso Labs**.

## 8. Format templates (the publishing kit)

One DNA, every surface. Each template is specified in the style guide.

| Format | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **OG / social card — dark** | 1200×630 | Ink Deep ground, Lora headline, mono dateline, release ledger or stamp, `S→S · from Enso Labs` masthead |
| **OG / social card — light** | 1200×630 | Paper ground, photo or stamp, editorial register |
| **LinkedIn carousel** | 1080×1350 | Cover (Lora headline + stamp) → atomic slides (one stat/idea each) → CTA. Highest-engagement format. |
| **Square post** | 1080×1080 | LinkedIn/X; headline + arrow device |
| **Newsletter header** | 1200×400 | Masthead + issue number (`v—` / `№`) + date |
| **/insights article** | responsive | Paper theme, Lora h1, mono meta, photo hero, coral links |
| **Signal card** | 1200×630 | Single ranked signal + confidence note + source (the engine's atomic unit) |

## 9. Migration: signal2noise → Strategy to Ship (staged)

Do **not** sweep the codebase yet — lock the brand first, then migrate in order:

1. **Lock** name, logotype, tokens (this doc + style guide). ← *we are here*
2. **New assets:** OG templates, wordmark, favicon/monogram.
3. **Code:** add `brand/strategy-to-ship/tokens.css`; introduce a `.theme-sts` (or fold into the existing `.theme-light` Insights theme).
4. **Content/site:** rename `signal2noise` → `Strategy to Ship` in `lib/site.ts`, `/insights`, components, RSS, schema, `CLAUDE.md`. Keep the `signal2noise` 301 history; never resurrect `signals.ensolabs.ai`.
5. **Pipelines:** rename the editorial-engine + scheduled tasks; update the publishing brief.

A tracked find-and-replace checklist ships with step 4 when you approve it.
